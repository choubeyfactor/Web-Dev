Samosa

Ingredients

  • Flour – Just regular all purpose/plain flour;
  • Ginger – Fresh ginger is best here, but you could substitute with ginger powder in an emergency 😇;
  • Peas – Frozen all the way! No need for fresh here; and
  • Coriander/cilantro – Stirred into the potato filling at the end, it adds such a great hit of freshness.
  • Potatoes – For the potato filling. Use either starchy or all-rounder potatoes, such as Sebago (Australia brushed “dirt” potatoes), Russet, Yukon Gold or Idahos (US), Maris Piper or King Edwards (UK);
  • Ghee or oil – Ghee is a traditional cooking fat used in Indian cooking. It is simply normal butter but with milk solids and water removed, leaving behind pure butter fat. Ghee has a more intense butter flavour than normal butter, with the added bonus that unlike butter, it doesn’t burn even on high heat. It is rubbed into the flour to make the Samosa pastry flaky. You can either make your own Ghee (it’s cheaper, really easy and keeps for months), buy it, or just use normal butter;
  • Green chilli – Use a cayenne pepper which provides just a mild background hum of spice. These Samosas are not overly spicy!

Instructions

  1. Make the dough by mixing flour, salt, oil and water. Knead well and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the filling by heating oil in a pan and sautéing cumin seeds, ginger, garlic, green chilli, onions and salt. Add boiled potatoes, peas, coriander leaves and spices and mash well.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into thin circles. Cut each circle into half and shape them into cones. Fill them with the potato mixture and seal the edges with water. Fry them in hot oil until golden and crisp.

Masala Dosa

Ingredients

  • Dosa batter: This is made by soaking rice, urad dal (black lentils), fenugreek seeds and salt overnight and then grinding them into a smooth paste. The batter is then fermented for 8 to 9 hours at room temperature until it becomes bubbly and sour.
  • Potato filling: This is made by boiling and mashing potatoes and then sautéing them with oil, mustard seeds, cumin seeds, chana dal (split chickpeas), onion, garlic, green chilli, curry leaves and turmeric powder. You can also add other spices like coriander powder, garam masala or red chilli powder for more flavor.
  • Chutney: This is a spicy dip that accompanies the dosa. There are many varieties of chutney such as coconut chutney, tomato chutney or peanut chutney. A common recipe for coconut chutney is to grind grated coconut, roasted gram dal (split chickpeas), green chilli, ginger, salt and water into a smooth paste and then temper it with oil, mustard seeds, urad dal and curry leaves.

Instructions

  1. Heat a dosa tawa/pan and smear some oil if using a cast-iron pan. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it thinly in a circular motion. Drizzle some oil or butter around the edges and cook until golden and crisp.
  2. Flip the dosa and cook on the other side for a few seconds. Then flip it back and place some potato filling on one half of the dosa. Fold the other half over the filling and press lightly.
  3. Transfer the masala dosa to a plate and serve hot with chutney

Kheer

Ingredients

  • Creamy Milk
  • Rice
  • Sugar

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 15 to 20 minutes. Drain the water and set aside1
  2. Boil milk in a large pot and add the rice. Cook on low-medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice is soft and the milk is thickened. This may take 40 to 50 minutes.
  3. Add sugar, cardamom powder and saffron soaked in warm milk. Mix well and simmer for another 10 minutes. Garnish with chopped nuts and raisins if desired
